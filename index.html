Global Unemployment

Unemployment Rate across the world

In the year of 2019, the world average unemployment rate has reached 7.11%, which is the lowest over the past 10 years (2011-2021).

The unemployment rate has fluctuated over the years.

As we can see from the graph, South Africa has the highest rate in unemployment with total 28.47%, which is most serious across the world.

Unemployment Rate by Region

The region Africa has the highest rate of unemployment.

This is followed by Asia, which has the second highest rate of unemployment by Region.

GDP per Capita vs Unemployment Rate

We can see that the country Luxembourg that with a high value of GDP per Capita, has a relatively low unemployment rate;

while country Sudan that with low value of GDP per Capita, has a relatively high unemployment rate.

Global Unemployment Rate across the year

In the year of 2019, the world average unemployment rate has reached 7.11%, which is the lowest over the past 10 years (2011-2021).

However, in the year of 2020, the world average unemployment rate has reached 8.31%, which is the highest over the past 10 years (2011-2021).

In conlucsion, the unemployment rate has fluctuated over the years.

Does Covid-19 affect global unemployment rate?

Here, we compare between year 2019 and 2020.

Year 2019 was before the pandemic, while year 2020 was during/after the pandemic.

We can see that the Top 20 higest unemployment rate countries have increased in terms of unemployment during and after the pandemic.
